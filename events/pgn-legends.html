PGN Inaugural Invitational Fall 2014 Rules.

Rules & Guidelines Poly Inaugural Invitational

Contact the following PGN officers for any questions regarding the tournament:

Skype: Sung Nho (ikalbi), Ryan Li (lir10005)

Email: rl1848@nyu.edu, polygamenetwork@gmail.com

Tournament Format

Game mode: Tournament Draft

  • Matches will be played throughout the month of October and November.
  • Games will be held on weekends (Friday-Sunday). Preferred series start times are at
  • 15 Minute breaks if needed but must confirm before lobby is made.
  • Teams are allowed to pause if a player disconnects. (Don't abuse the feature)
  • 2 Pauses per team. No longer than 10 Minutes.

Group Stages are TWO games

  • Winners of BOTH games receive 3 points
  • Ties result in 1 point per team
  • Losses result in 0 points
  • First and Second places advance to Playoff Stages
  • In case of a tie, a one-game Tiebreaker will be played. Coin toss to decide pick of sides.
  • You will be notified if your games will be streamed

Playoff Stages are Best of THREE

  • Coin flip by officials to decide choice of side
  • Playoff series will be streamed
  • There is NO Losers Bracket
  • Side alternates after each game

Grand Finals are Best of FIVE

  • Coin flip by officials to decide choice of side
  • If series proceeds to fifth game, it will be played in BLIND PICK format
  • Grand Finals will be held OFFLINE at NYU: SOE facilities. If you cannot make it, then contact officials to schedule and set up the ONLINE game. Of course, a LAN environment is preferred.
  • Side alternates after each game
  • Grand Finals will be streamed

Creating Team & Joining Tournament

Sign up on Google Docs

Eligibility

  • Your team must have at least 5 members on your roster up to 7 players including substitutes.
  • 2 subs are allowed (Or you will be given subs via the Free Agent forms)
  • Rosters CANNOT be changed once tournament starts barring catastrophe. Any changes must be supervised and approved by PGN officers.
  • Must be level 30 minimum. May be unranked. (Sorry pre-30s!)
  • If there are more signups than space for the 16 teams, you will be notified if you have been selected to participate
  • You must have at least 1 NYU student with a valid @nyu.edu email on the team. Teams with more than 1 NYU student will be given preference in the selection process. You may sign up as a free agent without being an NYU student, but you will be given lower priority in team creation.

Match Setup

  • Matches will be prescheduled for certain times. Failure to reschedule a day in advance or have available subs will result in a forfeit.
  • Matches will be set up by a PGN officer/volunteer. They will invite team captains who are responsible for inviting all of their members. This is ONLY if the matches are streamed. Otherwise teams are responsible for setting up the lobby and reporting results truthfully and reliably.
  • Passwords for the lobbies will be given out upon successful confirmation of team preparations from Team Captains.

Penalties / Forfeits / General Rules

  • Both teams have 5 minutes to get 5 players as soon as they are in the lobby unless an emergency substitution must be made. (Remember subs may be used at will since the roster consists of all 7 members)
  • If there are any issues at all, captains/members must report it to a PGN officer FIRST. Failure to disclose information to PGN officials first or posting of the issue on outlets (i.e. social media) before resolution may result in penalties/forfeits
  • Make sure to take screenshots if the other teams do not show and to verify game results. Failure to do so may result in reduction of earned points.
  • If more than the allotted pauses are used a team may lose bans, be given or forfeit or even disqualified.
  • If a team fails to adhere to the Rules, screenshots must be provided to back up the claims and then sent to a PGN officer for review.
  • Screenshots should be sent to the PGN email polygamenetwork@gmail.com with the subject, "PGN Tourney"

Post Match

Team Captains are obligated to submit post-match screenshots within 30 minutes of result if the game is not streamed. This applies to most Group Stage games. In the case of series, you may screenshot each result and then send the end of series results together.

Prizes

Please note that prizes can take up to several weeks to be distributed by Riot.

Riot will put the prizes directly into your League of Legends account. Make sure to keep your profile updated with your correct League of Legends summoner name.

Your screenshots must have matching names compared to your profile to receive your prize.

Additional prizes will be given to the First place team in the form of "Tons of Damage" Wristbands!

All rulings by PGN officers are final and must be complied with. Failure to do so may result in sanctions and disqualification.
